{% trans "Bet Groups Management" %}
{% blocktrans %} Create as many Bet Groups as you want and manage them separately. You can think of Bet Groups as wallets to which you make deposits and place your bets with. When you sign up in zakanda, a bet group is automatically created for you. This is your default bet group. Every time you make a deposit, you deposit to this bet group and when you bet, you use money from this bet group. You can create more bet groups if you want. For example, you can create one and name it "Outsiders". After its creation, its balance will be 0 no matter if you have money in your other bet groups. To put some money to this new bet group you have to make a new deposit to it. As soon as its balance is greater than 0, you can start using it for betting. Any time that you want to bet on an outsider, you can use the money of the "Surprises" bet group. This way you will be able to see how it performs over time without messing with your other groups.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Private Bet Amounts" %}
{% blocktrans %}In zakanda you have the option to keep your bet amounts and balance private, visible only by you. While you will be betting the amount of your choice, the others will not be able to see your real bet amounts. They will only see them as percentage of your balance. For example, if you have 1000 virtual euros in one of your bet groups and you make a bet using 100 of them, the others will see that you used 10 percent of your balance for that bet. In order for your balance to be private too, we convert it to what we call "units". A unit represents your average bet amount. For example if your balance is 1000 virtual euros, and your average bet is 10 euros, then the others will see that your balance is 100 units without knowing that your 100 units correspond to 1000 euros. This way you can be reassured that your money amounts remain always private.{% endblocktrans %}
{% blocktrans %}In the main menu bar under your profile picture, there is a switch with 2 states: Cash On and Cash Off. If you switch to Cash On, then zakanda shows the real bet amounts and balances if possible. So, if you are checking the history or the bets of a player who doesn't hide his money, you will see his real amounts. If you switch to "Cash Off" then you will see percentages and units.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Interactive Charts" %}
{% blocktrans %}One of the unique features of zakanda is its interactive statistics. The performance of a player is described not only by some indicators like ROI and Yield, but by a variety of charts that interact with each other. For example, in the Wins-Losses pie if you select the wins, then all the other charts will be filtered to describe the wins. This way you can see with just one click, which are the won matches, in which markets they correspond (over/under, double chance etc.) what is their average odd and stake and many more indicators that describe them. {% endblocktrans %}
